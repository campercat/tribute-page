Five Things to Consider Before Getting a Cat

In the US, 25.4% (source) of all households own cats, that's about 1 out of every 4 American households. For the families that have cats, the average number owned per household is 1.8 cats.

Both dogs and cats are very popular options for pets, they provide us love and affection and many owners treat their furry friends as part of their family. If you ever thought about or are currently considering adding a cat to your family, that is great! But, before you go out to buy or adopt a cat (you should always choose adopting over buying), you should probably consider the following 5 things before jumping in.

1. Time

Taking care of any animals require a lot of time and patience. As an owner, you will need to spend time with your cat so he/she doesn't feel lonely, such as playing with them, or just "talking" to them since cats tend to try to communicate with their humans using meows.

2. Maintenance

Having cats means more than just daily cleaning of the litter box. The addition of a cat to your household is lovely and your cat will give you unconditional love, but you are also responsible for all the work that comes with it. When cats use their litter box they usually have litter pieces tracking on the floor, then as the owner you might want to do cleaning around the house more often to minimize litter on the floor. Cats also shed very often, so don't be surprised when your clothes, and beddings are covered with your cat's hair!

3. Cost

You are probably aware of the cost for buying their food, litter, and regular check-ups to the Vet. But that is not all! In addition to the essentials, cats will also need accessories such as nail clippers, toys, furnitures, as well as supplementary food such as vitamins and other health products to maintain their health. Also, do not underestimate the cost to the vets, the bill can easily go well over $1000 because sometimes your cat might need to do various exams to pin point the health issue, especially when the cat gets older.

4. Commitment

Cats can live up to 20 years with proper care. In the the span of time that you both share, will you be able to provide sufficient care and attention to your cat? Will you be willing to look for someone else to take care of your cat when you are out of town for more than a day?

5. Emotions

You bring a cat home, take proper care of the cat and you guys share wonder times together. Many cat owners treat their cats the same as family and nothing less. However, cats get sick as they age, will you be ready to take the emotional responsibility when they pass away?

Being a pet owner means huge responsibility, but if you are up for the challenge, you will find yourself a funny/affectionate/adorable company for many years to come.
